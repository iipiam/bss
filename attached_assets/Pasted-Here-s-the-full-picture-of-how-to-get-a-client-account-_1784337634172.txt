Here's the full picture of how to get a client account live on ZATCA Phase 2, based on how your system is built.

What to collect from each client (before you start)
Business identity:

VAT registration number (15 digits, starts and ends with 3)
Commercial Registration (CR) number
Official company name (exactly as registered with ZATCA)
Branch name (if they have more than one branch)
National address (as registered with ZATCA):

Street name, building number, district (city subdivision), city, postal code
Access needed:

Login access to their Fatoora portal (fatoora.zatca.gov.sa) — or have the client sit with you, because they must generate a one-time OTP code from that portal during activation. The OTP is only valid for about an hour, so plan this step together.
Activation steps (done from your IT ZATCA Settings page)
Enter the client's profile — fill in the VAT number, CR number, company name, branch, and address, and pick the environment. Best practice: test on Simulation first, then redo on Production.

Generate CSR — one click. The system creates the client's private key and a certificate request. Nothing is sent to the client; it's stored securely per account.

Get the OTP and onboard — the client (or you, with their Fatoora login) logs into the Fatoora portal, clicks "Onboard new solution unit / device", and generates an OTP. Enter that OTP in your settings page and click Onboard. ZATCA returns the Compliance CSID (a temporary certificate).

Run compliance checks — one click. The system automatically sends sample standard and simplified invoices to ZATCA to prove the setup works. All must pass.

Get Production CSID — one click after checks pass. This exchanges the temporary certificate for the permanent production one.

Enable the integration — flip the toggle to Enabled. From then on, every invoice is automatically signed, gets the 9-tag QR code, and is reported to (simplified) or cleared by (standard) ZATCA.

Things to watch out for
The OTP expires quickly (~1 hour) — coordinate with the client so you enter it right after they generate it.
The company name and VAT number you enter must match ZATCA's records exactly, or onboarding will fail.
Each branch/device needs its own onboarding if they invoice separately.
If something goes wrong mid-way, you have a "reset onboarding" option that clears the certificates so you can start over.
Keep the client's certificates and secrets in the system only — never send them by email or WhatsApp.
So in short: collect VAT number, CR number, exact registered name, national address, and Fatoora portal access — then the whole activation is about 10 minutes of clicking through the 4 steps on your ZATCA Settings page.
